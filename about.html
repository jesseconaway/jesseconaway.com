ABOUT ME

I enjoy solving novel problems in creative, engaging ways. Each problem is novel at least in some small way, and that’s what leads to differences in design solutions. When that solution is creative and engaging, it’s more likely to make people think, and take action. Good design causes change.

SUPER SECRET RAMEN RECIPE

This recipe is product of my quest to reproduce the NongShim brand Shin Ramyun ramen noodles that I grew up with as a kid. The South Korean company NongShim first introduced this specific style of instant ramen noodle soup in 1986. My parents lived in Korea for a brief time in 1990, and as a result, the Shin Ramyun instant noodles were an ever-present piece of my childhood.

The problem for me is that the noodles are deep-fried in palm oil, which is not only unhealthy, but also contributes to many environmental issues surrounding the production of palm oil.

This ramen, recreated in this recipe, is my ultimate comfort food, and it's my gift to you for visiting my site. Enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 1/4 c. Soy Sauce
  • 1 tbsp. Better Than Bouillon - Low Sodium Chicken Flavor
  • 5 c. Water
  • 2 tsp. Chili Garlic Sauce (I used the same brand as Sriracha sauce)
  • 1 tsp. Ginger Powder
  • 1 tst. Chili Powder
  • 1 tbsp. Oyster Sauce
  • 1/3 c. Dehydrated Shitake Mushrooms
  • 1 tsp. Minced Garlic
  • 8-10oz Ramen / Chuka Soba Noodles (These can be hard to find, but make sure they're made from wheat flower, not buckwheat or rice, and try to get low sodium noodles.)
  • 4 Eggs (optional)

Cook the noodles as instructed on the package, usually 4 minutes at a boil, followed by rinsing under cold water. Try to under-shoot the time just a little, and leave the noodles a little firm, because we'll put them back into the hot broth.

Combine all ingredients except the noodles and eggs into a large pot or wok, and bring to a low boil to make the broth.

Crack the eggs into the broth, and immediately add the noodles to the broth, covering the eggs to cook. Do not stir or otherwise disturb the mix: let the eggs cook for 5-7 minutes at a low boil, depending on how you like your eggs. I recommend over-medium, so err on the shorter side of cook time. If you hate runny yolks, let those puppies simmer for a bit.

And that's it! You can jazz it up with some meat, sliced carrot, or green onions, but I like to keep it simple.
